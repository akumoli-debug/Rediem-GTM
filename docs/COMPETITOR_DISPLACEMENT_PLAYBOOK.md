# Competitor Displacement Playbook

Rediem displacement intelligence should explain the wedge, not start a tool fight. The output should preserve the buyer's current stack and show where Rediem adds a verified participation layer across loyalty, reviews, subscriptions, CRM, SMS, support, mobile, and commerce.

## Rule Of Thumb

- Do not lead with replacement language.
- Do not frame Rediem as a loyalty, review, CRM, subscription, SMS, helpdesk, mobile app, or ecommerce platform swap.
- Do name the existing tool as evidence of current motion.
- Do explain the next participation loop Rediem can unlock.
- Do route events back into the systems the team already uses.

## Common Stack Combinations

| Detected stack | Recommended angle |
| --- | --- |
| LoyaltyLion or Smile plus Okendo, Yotpo, Reviews.io, or Stamped | Reviews and loyalty appear separate. Rediem connects proof, referral, reward, and community actions without asking the team to rip out either surface. |
| Klaviyo plus loyalty or reviews | Rediem feeds verified participation events into lifecycle segmentation, so CRM flows can respond to actions beyond orders and clicks. |
| Recharge plus loyalty or reviews | Rediem adds participation rewards around subscriber milestones, replenishment, reviews, referrals, and education. |
| Shopify plus any retention stack | Shopify remains the commerce system of record. Rediem adds verified participation data around it. |
| Attentive or Postscript plus Klaviyo | Rediem creates higher-intent reasons to message across CRM and SMS while respecting the existing channel stack. |
| Gorgias plus reviews or loyalty | Support moments can become trust-building follow-up loops after the issue is resolved. |
| Tapcart plus loyalty, reviews, or SMS | App engagement can become participatory through verified challenges, drops, referrals, reviews, and rewards. |

- `detected_current_stack`

## QA Checklist

- The copy avoids generic rip-and-replace positioning.
- The wedge names Rediem's participation layer, verified events, or community loop.
- The recommendation uses the detected vendor as context, not as an enemy.
- The buyer persona is close to retention, lifecycle, ecommerce, loyalty, CX, or community ownership.
- Evidence IDs and source URLs are preserved when available.
